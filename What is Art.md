Rabindranath Tagore, in his essay “What is Art?”, presents art as the natural expression of human emotion rather than only an imitation of the outer world. He argues that art is born of the artist's inner feelings and experiences, looking for form and communicating itself to others. According to Tagore, art is not confined to technical skill or strictly set rules; instead, it is a living process through which individuals express their deepest joys, sorrows, and points of view. This emotional originality makes art meaningful and allows it to resonate universally across cultures and time.

Tagore further focuses on the fact that art is a form of communication that connects individuals beyond the limitations of language and logic. It serves as a connecting bridge between the creator's inner world and the audience's outer world. Through art, personal emotions are translated into shared human experiences, enabling others to feel and understand what the artist has felt. In this sense, art plays a vital role in creating empathy and unity among people, as it jumps beyond social, cultural, and intellectual walls of limitation.

Another key idea in Tagore’s essay is that art is closely linked to the concept of freedom. He believes that true art cannot progress under strict rules or mechanical imitation, as it requires the ability to think on the spot and sincerity. When art becomes overly concerned with perfection, imitation, or adherence to established conventions, it loses its truthfulness. Tagore advises a more organic approach to artistic creation, where the artist remains true to their inner voice and allows their emotions to guide their work. This freedom is what gives art its originality and power.

Finally, Tagore views art as an essential and important aspect of human life that makes both the individual and society rich and full of depth. It is not merely decorative or entertaining but deeply connected to the spiritual and emotional development of human beings. Art reflects the harmony between one’s self and the universe, revealing deeper truths about existence. By engaging with art, individuals become more aware of their own feelings and the shared human condition. Thus, Tagore concludes that art is a vital force that nurtures creativity, understanding, and a sense of unity in the world.
