## Short Question Answer

**Q:1 What did the fishermen decide to do?**

**Ans:** The fishermen who came to the lake (Phullotpala) decided to stay at the lake that day. They also decided to catch the fish, the tortoises and other creatures in the morning. This gave time to the fish and the tortoise to think of ways to escape to a safe place. The fishermen might be too tired to do the job or they might be in a mood to rest and relax for the night. 

---

**Q:2 Who was Ratnaprabha? What impression do you form of her character ?**

**Ans:** Ratnaprabha was the wife of a merchant named Samudra Dutt in Vikramapura. She fell in love with one of her servants.It shows that she was unfaithful to her husband.When she was caught red-handed while kissing her servant lover ,she did not lose her cool at all. She was quick-witted.She at once blamed the servant for stealing camphor and other luxury items, adding that she discovered the fact by smelling his mouth.She saved herself and her lover.In short, she was unfaithful but quick -witted and clever. 

---

**Q:3 What fate did Quick-Wit and Fatalist eventually meet?** 

**Ans:** The Quick-Wit was a fish named Pratyutpannamati , and the Fatalist was another fish named Yadbavishya. Quick-Wit was caught in the net in the morning. He was very clever and quick. He pretended to be dead, and on being taken out of the net, he jumped and dived into deep water and thus saved himself from being killed and consumed. On the other hand, the Fatalist who believed in ’What will be, will be’ did nothing. He was caught by the fishermen and was killed. 

---

**Q:4 Why was the pair of cranes grief- stricken?** 

**Ans:** On a fig tree by the side of Airavati river lived some cranes. Beneath their tree there was a burrow, in which a serpent lived. The serpent was in the habit of eating the cranes’ young babies. Thus, the pair of cranes whose children were eaten by the serpent were griefstricken.They lamented over their death and wailed loudly.

**Q:5 why did not the vulture think it appropriate for the crow to be made the king of Karpura island?** 

**Ans:** Dapple wanted the crow to be made the king of Karpura island as he had done a good job by setting fire to the fort, because a servant’s work should not go unnoticed and unrewarded. The Vulture, the chief minister, did not approve of the idea. According to the Vulture, base person or low creature should never be appointed to an office meant for the noble because such a person or creature could think of even killing their master, as happened in the case of the hermit and his mouse. The mouse who was turned into a tiger thought of killing the hermit out of arrogance. 

---

**Q:6 What was Mahatapa’s folly?** 

**Ans:** Mahatapa Was a hermit. One day a baby mouse fell near his hermitage from the clutches of a crow. He began to nurture it with grains of wild rice. When a cat ran after it to catch and eat it, it jumped into the hermit’s lap. The hermit did a folly by turning it into a cat so that it could live safely. He committed another folly by turning it into a dog and then another by turning it into a tiger. A mouse does not deserve to be a tiger. In the tiger’s state the mouse was about to kill the hermit , the hermit turn it again into a mouse.

**Q:1 What does “the Quick-Witted Wife” say about womenfolk? Do you agree with it? Why or why not ?** 

**Ans:** In the story ‘The Quick-Witted Wife’, the narrator remarks that no man is dear and no man is repulsive or displeasing to women. According to the narrator ,women are like cattle which like to seek new grass to chew and they want to take to new lovers as they are never satisfied. This is certainly a controversial and derogatory opinion about women in general. First of all, men can be as unfaithful as women. Men can be as unfaithful to their wives as their wives to them. Secondly, we cannot generalise all women as unfaithful. Even in history there were women who preferred death over adultery. Who can forget the story of Sita in the epic Ramayana-Sita who refused to submit to Ravana, despite immense pressure? The legendary Queen Padmavati burnt herself to save her honour from the invader, Alauddin Khilji. It is ironic that many scholars even in our times dub women as more unfaithful and more wavering in than men in general.

**Q:2 What solution did the tortoise propose in order to leave the lake? Was it a good fix? Give reasons for your answer.** 

**Ans:** In the story ‘The Foolish Tortoise’, we Find that the tortoise considered himself to be a good planner. That is why, in order to escape from the net of the fishermen, he proposes a plan to go to another lake. He asks his friends, the two Swans, to help him reach the second lake by the aerial route. The Swans were surprised and asked how it was possible. The tortoise said that he could hang by his mouth from a piece of wood both the Swans held up with their beaks and with the power of their wings he would be able to reach the second lake without difficulty. This proposal of the tortoise was not a good one. It is even ridiculous to think as to how to tortoise could hang from a piece of wood. The mouth- grip of the tortoise could weaken, leading to his fatal fall. Or the Swans might not be able to fly comfortably with the weight of the tortoise hanging in the middle. All the three could fall and die. Another difficulty in the process was indicated by the Swans themselves. They said that when people saw that tortoise being carried by the Swans, they would surely make comments. If the tortoise gave any reply on hearing them , he would fall and be killed. So the Swans advised him to stay where he was. The tortoise forced them to do as he wanted. When people made some comments he ignored the good advice of the friends, and reacted in anger. Consequently, he fell down as he opened his mouth, was killed and consumed. The solution to the tortoise ‘s problem was planned but not well-planned ,because the tortoise in his foolishness did not think about the difficulties in the way of his plan and forgot the advice of his friends at the crucial moment.

**Q:3 Comment on the choice of names for various characters in the text. What symbolic significance do they carry?** 

**Ans:** The names of various characters in the Hitopadesha stories are symbolic, often reflects the nature, virtues or vices of the characters they represent, serving to highlight moral lessons. In the story, ‘Short-sighted Crane ‘,the crow Cloudcolour symbolises cunning, strategic planning and untrustworthy nature. He is called the king of crows who plots against the owl Kingdom. In the story he sets the fire to the fort which was neglected for long. The Vulture as the chief minister in the story has also symbolic meaning. It represents foresight, wisdom and diplomatic prudence. That is why the Vulture cautious the king against assigning an office meant for a noble to a base person. In the same story, the crane symbolises foolishness and shortsightedness. It does not perceive the danger inherent in the plan to attract the mongooses to kill the serpent. In the story ‘The Foolish Tortoise’, The tortoise symbolises foolishness, arrogance and overconfidence. That is why he meets his doom ignoring the necessary precautions for safety. The Swans symbolises prudence and foresight. They warn the tortoise against making plans without thinking of the dangers. In the story ‘The Hermit and the Mouse’, the hermit symbolises wisdom, compassion and the power of knowledge. He possesses the power to alleviate the lowly to a position of power and also brings down the powerful to a lowly position, as he does in the case of turning the mouse into the tiger and then again turning the tiger back into the mouse to show him his true status. The mouse, represents the volatile, ego- driven nature of a person, who when given power becomes arrogant and dangerous. In the story’The Three Fish’,the fish ,Anagata Vidhata, Pratyutpannamati and Yadbhavishya symbolise three different approaches to life. Angata Vidhata represents foresight and proactive planning and safety. Pratyutpannamati represents presence of mind, adaptability and quick decision. Yadbhavishya represents fatalism,or belief in blind luck.No wonder, the first two fish save themselves from danger, while the third is caught and killed. In ‘The Quick-Witted Wife’, Ratnaprabha symbolises adultery , cleverness , intelligence and quick wit , while her husband Samudra Dutt represents naivety. While Ratnaprabha is able to befool her husband , her husband believes her blindly. In short, Descriptors in the stories of Hitopadesha have symbolic meaning. They represent human traits and are used to guide humans in different avenues of life.

**Q:4 Summarise the lessons underlying the selected stories ?** 

**Ans:** The stories of Hitopadesha convey some lessons to guide us in different walks of life. The underlying lesson of the story ‘The Foolish Tortoise ‘ ,is that we should toy take into consideration the necessary precaution while making a plan. We should not ignore the good advice of friends. We should also not be overconfident. The tortoise, ignoring the advice of his friends, the two Swans, meets his doom. ‘The Three Fish’ Provides us an insight into three different attitudes or outlooks in life- the one of foresight and proactivity ,the second of presence of mind and adaptability and third of blind reliance on fate and inactivity. The story, conveys that a passive attitude and blind faith in destiny-‘What will be ,will be’- leads to destruction. ‘The Quick-Witted Wife’ underlines The message that those with intelligence and quickness of mind can befool others easily and that we should not be naive enough to believe whatever others say. ‘The Short-sighted Crane’ conveys The lesson that while planning we must think of the problems we can face in the way, otherwise we may be inviting trouble for ourselves. The story also underlines a significant message for prudence in politics and administration. We must praise or reward a subordinate for any good work done but we should be careful in giving any office or position of power to an undeserving person as that can boomerang on us in the end. ‘The Hermit and the Mouse’ teaches That even a wise and intelligent person should not commit the folly of empowering a base person. We should be wise and compassionate but careful also.
