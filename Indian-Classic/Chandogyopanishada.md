## Short Question Answer

**Q:1 How is the spirit explained to be thoroughly independent?**

**Ans:** The Chandogyopanishada presents The spirit as thoroughly independent. The spirit is identified with Brahman( the absolute), the ultimate reality that exists on its own. It supports everything else and needs no support for itself. It is free from the limitations of the body- it is the body that needs it and not the true self or the spirit that needs the body. Moreover, unlike the human body, the spirit or the true self is untouched by sorrow, decay, old age or death. 

---

**Q:2 How does the text establish the significance of Spirit over the transience of physical bonds and relationship ?** 

**Ans:** According to the Chandogyopanishada , Physical attraction and material- based relationships are impermanent. They are subject to the limitations of the body and changing circumstances. So long as the spirit is in the body there is some relationship. As soon as the spirit departs there is no relationship.’ Father ,mother, brother, sister’- all these terms lose their meaning when the spirit, or the self which is permanent departs. The body remains, which is burnt or buried, as per the rituals. It is the spirit which is equated with the infinite. 

---

**Q:3 Who is a ‘high-talker’? Why should he not conceal this fact?** 

**Ans:** The Chandogyopanishada describes a’ high- talker’ as one who has realised the spirit as the Lord of all in his own self. He has thought and perceived all things ,mobile and mobile and all human bonds and relationships as spirit encompassing. He has thus become capable of talking of things beyond things Beginning with Name and ending with Hope. The ‘high-talker’ should not conceal the fact of his being so ,because he has realised the spirit as his own self. If he talks high, of course, he talks high by the True(God).
